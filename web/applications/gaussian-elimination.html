Gaussian Elimination

Gaussian elimination is a standard method for solving systems of linear equations. The input is a system of the form $$\begin{align} a_{11} x_1 + \dotsb + a_{1n} x_n &= b_1 \\ &\vdots \\ a_{m1} x_1 + \dotsb + a_{mn} x_n &= b_m \end{align}$$ and we will gradually transform it into $$\begin{align} x_1 &= c_1 \\ &\vdots \\ x_m &= c_m \end{align}$$ The basic operation we will use is to replace one equation by the linear combination of two equations. Suppose we know that $e=f$ and $g=h$, where $e,f,g,h$ are some expressions. Then, we also know that $\lambda_1 e + \lambda_2 g = \lambda_1 f + \lambda_2 h$, for all numbers $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$. And, in fact, if $\lambda_1 \ne 0$, then $$\begin{align} \lambda_1 e + \lambda_2 g &= \lambda_1 f + \lambda_2 h \\ g &=h \end{align}$$ contain as much information as $$\begin{align} e &= f \\ g &=h \end{align}$$ Indeed, observe that the transformation is reversible, by an appropriate choice of coefficients.

We will use such transformation to first transform the system into one which has zeros below the main diagonal; that is, $a_{ij}=0$ if $i \gt j$. In a second phase, we will obtain the final form.
